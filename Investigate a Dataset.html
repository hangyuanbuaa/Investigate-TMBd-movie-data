Introduction¶

The dataset to be analyzed here is collected from 'The Movie Database (TMDb)', already partially cleaned from original data on Kaggle, it contains information about 10,000 movies such as user ratings and budget/revenue.
The following questions about this dataset are raised here, with respect to the development trend of film industry and the correlation between financial performances, user ratings, and possible affecting factors.

Questions:¶

  1. Is there a high rate of financial failures or success?
  2. Does the film industry become more and more profitable on the average over years?

  3. How do the viewer's ratings of movies change over years?

  4. Which genres are most popular?

  5. Do comercially successful movies always have higher ratings?
  6. What kinds of properties are associated with movies that have high revenues?

Q4: Which genres are most popular? From year to year?
To investigate which genres are most popular, two approaches are undertaken:

  1. the average popularity of each genre overall is used as indicator to find out which genres are most popular in a whole, and the corresponding average ratings and revenues are used as additional information;
  2. the numbers of movies produced in all genre categories over years are used as a indicator for the decision-making results of the film industry, in order to fulfill the viewers' need.

Approach 1: Use the average popularity of each genre overall as indicator to find out which genres are most popular in a whole, and the corresponding average ratings and revenues are used as additional information.

In [29]:
# first, the data inside column 'genres' are categorical, most movies belong to more than one genre, 
# so to access the categorical variables, the so called 'dummy matrix' or 'indicator matrix' are being introduced here.
# the original column will be divided into multiple columns, each represents a category, in the cells are 1 or 0
# using 1 to indicate that the movie belongs to this specific category. [2]
def indicator_matrix(df, col):
    col_iter = (set(x.split('|')) for x in col)
    categories = sorted(set.union(*col_iter))
    indicators = pd.DataFrame(np.zeros((len(df), len(categories))), columns = categories)
    for i, gen in enumerate(col):
        indicators.loc[i,gen.split('|')]=1
    return indicators

Conclusions¶

After cleaned up the TMDb dataset (The Movie Database), trend of film industry and correlations between movie performance with possible affecting factors are investigated here. The film industry is quite profitable, with higher rate success than failure, however the average profit factor and viewers' rating are decreasing year by year. Action movies are most popular among all the genres over years. Commercially successful movies tend to have higher ratings. Movies released in winter seem to have higher average revenue. Correlations between runtime/budget and movie performance are week. The correlations between cast/production company to their movies are quite strong, however director seems to play a less important role.
